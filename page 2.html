Top 5 Poses

1 - Seated Meditation Pose (Sukhasana): This is a basic and versatile pose. Sit cross-legged on a cushion or a mat with your spine erect. You can use a meditation cushion (zafu) or a folded blanket to support your hips and maintain a comfortable seated position.

2 - Half-Lotus Meditation Pose (Ardha Padmasana): In this pose, one foot rests on the opposite thigh, while the other foot is tucked in close to the body. This pose offers more stability than the cross-legged position and is commonly used in traditional meditation practices.

3 - Full Lotus Meditation Pose (Padmasana): This pose requires flexibility and is considered one of the most stable positions for meditation. Both feet are placed on the opposite thighs, with the soles facing upward. It helps promote a grounded and centered posture.

4 - Kneeling Meditation Pose (Seiza): Kneel on a cushion or meditation bench, keeping your knees slightly apart. Rest your buttocks on your heels, and keep your spine straight. This pose is an excellent alternative for those who find sitting cross-legged uncomfortable.

5 - Reclining Meditation Pose (Shavasana): Lie flat on your back, with your legs extended and your arms by your sides. This pose is often used for relaxation and body awareness meditations. It is particularly suitable for individuals who have difficulty sitting for long periods.
